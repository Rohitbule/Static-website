Misal Pav

Ingredients

  • Sprouted moth beans (matki)
  • Onion Tomato Garlic
  • shrigajananmasale Turmeric - As per the taste
  • shrigajananmasale Seka Cumin Powder - ¼ tea spoon
  • shrigajananmasale Red chili powder
  • shrigajananmasale Garam masala powder
  • shrigajananmasale Lal Mirch Powder - ¼ teaspoon

Method

  1. Wash and soak the sprouted moth beans for 4-5 hours
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them crackle.Add turmeric powder, red chilli powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  3. Add the sprouted moth beans and mix well. Add enough water to cover the sprouts and let it come to a boil
  4. Reduce the flame and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes till the sprouts are cooked and the curry thickens.
  5. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and sev. Serve hot with pav.

Mutton

Ingredients

  • Sprouted moth beans (matki)
  • Onion Tomato Garlic
  • 500 gms Mutton Pieces
  • 4-5 medium Onion
  • 4-5 medium Coconut Pieces
  • 1 tbsp Ginger garlic paste by shrigajananmasale
  • 1 cup Coriander Leaves
  • 2-4 Green Chillies
  • 4 tbsp Curd
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala by shrigajananmasale
  • Salt according to taste

Method

  1. In a bowl take the mutton pieces and add ginger garlic, curd, and a green paste made by blending coriander leaves and chilies together.
  2. Let this marinate in the green paste for a minimum of 30 minutes and a maximum overnight if you have the time.
  3. Take the whole onions and coconut pieces and roast them slightly on the gas stove.
  4. Keep an eye on the coconut and onions; make sure they are lightly charred but not completely black.
  5. Grind this into a paste with minimum or no oil.
  6. In a pressure cooker, add the marinated mutton and let it cook on high flame for the first 6-8 minutes. Continuously keep stirring at this point.
  7. Once the fat of the mutton is reduced a little, add the black paste and stir some more.
  8. Add salt according to your preference and a cup of hot water. Let this cook on low heat for 4 whistles.
  9. Simmer for another 5 minutes, and switch off the flame.
  10. Once the steam has evaporated naturally, open the cooker and enjoy hot.
